A unified research effort comprising a benchmark dataset, a structuring framework, and the LunguageScore metric. Abstract

Radiology reports convey detailed clinical observations and capture diagnostic reasoning that evolves over time. However, existing evaluation methods are limited to single-report settings and rely on coarse metrics that fail to capture fine-grained clinical semantics and temporal dependencies. We introduce LUNGUAGE, a benchmark dataset for structured radiology report generation that supports both single-report evaluation and longitudinal patient-level assessment across multiple studies. It contains 1,473 annotated chest X-ray reports, each reviewed by experts, and 80 of them contain longitudinal annotations to capture disease progression and inter-study intervals, also reviewed by experts. Using this benchmark, we develop a two-stage structuring framework that transforms generated reports into fine-grained, schema-aligned structured reports, enabling longitudinal interpretation. We also propose LUNGUAGESCORE, an interpretable metric that compares structured outputs at the entity, relation, and attribute level while modeling temporal consistency across patient timelines. These contributions establish the first benchmark dataset, structuring framework, and evaluation metric for sequential radiology reporting, with empirical results demonstrating that LUNGUAGESCORE effectively supports structured report evaluation.

Key Contributions

  • Novel structuring paradigm capturing clinician‑level nuance with fine‑grained entity, attribute, and reasoning layers.
  • Patient‑level longitudinal linking via ENTITYGROUP and TEMPORALGROUP that models diagnostic evolution over time.
  • Radiologist‑verified schema designed from clinical practice first principles (17k entities; 23k relation–attribute pairs).
  • LunguageScore: a unified metric integrating semantic, structural, and temporal fidelity for patient‑level evaluation.
  • Comprehensive benchmark across single and longitudinal settings with recent vision–language models.

Evaluation Metric: LunguageScore

A patient‑level evaluation integrating semantic alignment, temporal consistency, and structural fidelity. Designed to correlate with radiologist judgments and generalize across models.

MatchScore = Semantic × Temporal × Structural

Semantic: cosine(embedding_pred, embedding_ref)
Temporal: weight(same_study, same_TEMPORALGROUP)
Structural: Σ_attr w_attr × sim(attr_pred, attr_ref)
